--- layout: default title: Publications description: Yuki Miura's postdoctoral research at Stanford University focused on reverse-engineering human neural circuit development and investigating neuropsychiatric disorders using stem cell-based organoid and assembloid technologies. Key achievements include: Development of Cortico-Striatal Assembloids: Created a platform to model the cortico-striatal pathway by fusing cortical and striatal organoids. This approach enabled studies of neuronal connectivity and dysfunction, particularly in neurodevelopmental disorders like Phelan-McDermid syndrome​​. Thalamocortical Circuit Models: Established human thalamocortical circuit models to analyze cellular and circuit-level abnormalities caused by pathogenic genetic variants, such as those in the CACNA1G gene associated with epilepsy and schizophrenia​​. Loop Assembloid Models: Pioneered a loop-shaped assembloid model simulating the cortico-striatal-thalamic-cortical network. This system was used to study the effects of genetic mutations, such as ASH1L, on neuronal synchronization in autism and Tourette syndrome​​. Disease Modeling and Functional Analysis: Utilized advanced tools like optogenetics, calcium imaging, and electrophysiological recordings to dissect neuronal activity in disease conditions. These platforms provided insights into the development of neural circuits and their dysfunctions​​. Overall, Yuki Miura's postdoctoral work significantly advanced the use of human stem cell-derived models for exploring the formation and pathology of neural circuits, offering new avenues for understanding and treating neuropsychiatric disorders. page_cover: '/images/100.jpg' ---
